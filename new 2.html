5,475 fresh Covid cases, toll stays under 100 for 2nd day

DurgeshNandanJha
@timesgroup.com

New Delhi: The daily death toll due to covid-19 in Delhi remained below 100 for the second consecutive day on Thursday even as new cases registered a marginal increase from 5,246 on Wednesday to 5,475 on Thursday.

In the past 24 hours, 91 peple died compared with 99 on Wednesday, the state health department said. The total deaths have now reached 8,811. Of these, 2,300 took place in November, the highest in a month till date. June had seen 2,269 deaths due to the viral disease.

The number of daily deaths crossed the 100 mark at least seven times between November12 and 24. the highest fatalities of 131 recorded in a single day was on November 18. In all, 1,64,556 new cases have been reported in the past 26 days, averaging 6,329 cases daily. The total tally has crossed 5.51 lakh.

On the brighter said, the positivity rate--number of positive cases amoung those tested - has reduced to less the 10% over the last two days. Data from the state health department showed that in the past 24 hours, 63,266 people were tested for Covid-19, the highest till date for the city of which 8.65% came posotive. Of the total test conducted, a record 28,897 were done by the RTPCR/CBNAAT/TrueNat.
The city had recorded its highest single-day spike of 8,593 cases on November 11. In all, 6,746 new cases were recorded on Sunday, 4,454 on Monday, 6,225 on Tuesday and 5,246 on Wednesday.
